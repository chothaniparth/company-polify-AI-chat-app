ACME CORPORATION — EMPLOYEE POLICY HANDBOOK
Version 3.2 | Effective January 1, 2025

SECTION 1: WORK HOURS & ATTENDANCE

1.1 Standard Work Hours
Standard office hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday. Core hours — when all team members must be available — are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Employees may flex their start and end times outside core hours with manager approval.

1.2 Remote Work Policy
Employees are eligible for up to 3 days of remote work per week after completing 90 days of employment. Remote work requests must be submitted and approved in the HR portal at least 24 hours in advance. Full remote work arrangements require VP-level approval and are reviewed quarterly.

1.3 Attendance & Punctuality
Employees are expected to be punctual. More than 3 unexcused tardies in a 30-day period will trigger a formal performance review. Absences must be reported to your direct manager before 9:30 AM on the day of the absence.

SECTION 2: LEAVE POLICIES

2.1 Annual Leave (PTO)
- Years 0–2: 15 days per year
- Years 3–5: 18 days per year
- Years 6+: 22 days per year
PTO accrues monthly and up to 5 unused days may be carried over to the next calendar year. Unused PTO beyond 5 days is forfeited on December 31.

2.2 Sick Leave
Employees receive 10 sick days per calendar year. Sick days do not carry over and are not paid out upon separation. A doctor's note is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

2.3 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers receive 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 6 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of birth, adoption, or placement.

2.4 Bereavement Leave
Employees receive up to 5 paid days for the loss of an immediate family member (spouse, parent, child, sibling). Up to 2 paid days are provided for extended family members (grandparents, in-laws, aunts/uncles).

2.5 Public Holidays
ACME observes all national public holidays plus 2 floating holidays per year that employees may use at their discretion with 48 hours' notice.

SECTION 3: CODE OF CONDUCT

3.1 Professionalism
All employees must maintain a respectful, inclusive, and professional environment. Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind — based on race, gender, religion, nationality, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic — is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination.

3.2 Confidentiality
Employees must protect the confidentiality of ACME's proprietary information, trade secrets, client data, and internal communications. Signing of the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is mandatory on the first day of employment.

3.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any outside employment, investments, or relationships that may create a conflict of interest with ACME's business. Written disclosure must be submitted to HR and approved before engaging in any such activity.

3.4 Social Media Policy
Employees are expected to be responsible on social media. Sharing confidential company information, disparaging clients, or misrepresenting your association with ACME online is grounds for disciplinary action. If you post publicly about work, include the disclaimer: "Views are my own."

SECTION 4: COMPENSATION & BENEFITS

4.1 Salary Reviews
Performance-based salary reviews are conducted annually every April. Merit increases are based on individual performance ratings and company financial health. Off-cycle adjustments require written justification and CHRO approval.

4.2 Bonus Structure
Employees are eligible for an annual performance bonus ranging from 5% to 20% of base salary, paid in March based on the prior year's results. New employees hired after October 1 are not eligible for that year's bonus cycle.

4.3 Health Insurance
ACME covers 100% of the employee premium and 60% of dependent premiums for medical, dental, and vision insurance. Open enrollment is held each November. New employees must enroll within 30 days of their start date.

4.4 Retirement Plan (401k)
ACME offers a 401(k) plan with a 4% company match (up to 4% of your salary). Employees are eligible to enroll after 60 days of employment. The company match vests over a 3-year cliff vesting schedule.

4.5 Learning & Development
Each employee receives an annual L&D budget of $1,500 for courses, books, certifications, and conferences. Unused funds do not roll over. Requests must be submitted in the HR portal and approved by your manager.

SECTION 5: IT & DATA SECURITY

5.1 Device Usage
Company-issued devices are for business use primarily. Personal use should be minimal and must not interfere with work performance. Installing unauthorized software on company devices is prohibited.

5.2 Password Policy
All passwords must be at least 14 characters and include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Passwords must be changed every 90 days. Use of a company-approved password manager is strongly encouraged.

5.3 Data Classification
Data is classified as: Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted. Employees must handle data according to its classification level. Restricted data (e.g., payroll, health records) must never be stored on personal devices or shared via unencrypted channels.

5.4 Incident Reporting
Any suspected security incident, data breach, or loss of a company device must be reported to the IT Security team within 2 hours of discovery at security@acme.com or extension 5555.

SECTION 6: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

6.1 Performance Review Cycle
Formal performance reviews are held twice per year: mid-year (July) and year-end (December). Reviews use a 5-point rating scale: Exceptional, Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory.

6.2 Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
Employees rated "Needs Improvement" for two consecutive reviews will be placed on a formal PIP. PIPs are 60-day structured plans with specific measurable goals. Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in termination.

6.3 Promotions
Promotions are tied to performance reviews and business need. Employees must have at least 12 months in their current role and a minimum rating of "Exceeds Expectations" in their last two reviews to be eligible.

SECTION 7: SEPARATION & OFFBOARDING

7.1 Resignation
Employees are expected to provide a minimum of 2 weeks' written notice. Senior roles (Director and above) require 4 weeks' notice. ACME reserves the right to accept notice immediately and process same-day separation.

7.2 Final Pay
Final paychecks including all owed compensation are processed within 3 business days of the last working day in accordance with applicable labor law.

7.3 Return of Property
All company equipment, access cards, and materials must be returned on or before the last day of employment. Failure to return equipment may result in deductions from the final paycheck as permitted by law.

7.4 Post-Employment Obligations
The NDA and non-solicitation clauses remain in effect for 12 months following separation. Employees may not solicit ACME clients or recruit ACME employees during this period.
